# whether.work Product Review: Add / Remove / Change Plan

This review converts your recommendations into a product-execution plan optimized for Whether's core loop:

**signals → posture → decision → artifact**

---

## Executive recommendation

Strengthen whether.work by making the homepage a decisive weekly operating brief, then layer trust and transmission:

1. **Timeline + confidence + drivers first** (credibility)
2. **Bounded action playbooks + founder risk check next** (decision quality)
3. **Personalized posture + share card exports** (stickiness and growth)

Do these in that order to avoid becoming a dashboard or a macro-content site.

---

## 1) What to ADD

## A. Historical Signal Timeline (P0)

### Why it matters
- Validates that posture calls move with real market cycles.
- Answers: "Can I trust this signal over time?"
- Converts current posture from opinion to observable track record.

### Product shape
- Add a compact timeline module to the homepage under the current posture card.
- Show annual and quarterly posture history (e.g., Defensive → Guarded → Expansion).
- Overlay 3–4 macro proxies (VC funding, layoffs, IPO activity, SaaS multiples).

### Decision impact
- Improves trust and lowers re-litigation in leadership meetings.

---

## B. Confidence Score + Trend (P0)

### Why it matters
- Signals uncertainty honestly.
- Makes a posture actionable without pretending precision.

### Product shape
- Add `Confidence: 68%` and `Trend: Improving / Stable / Deteriorating` directly beside posture.
- Include one-line provenance: "Based on X indicators updated weekly."

### Decision impact
- Better pacing decisions when confidence is low (slower changes) vs high (faster execution).

---

## C. Driver Transparency (P0)

### Why it matters
- Founders need to know what moved, not just the score.
- Enables "what would flip this call" conversations.

### Product shape
- Add 3–5 ranked drivers with direction arrows (e.g., "IPO window weak ↓").
- Keep to one-screen summary first; deep explainer remains secondary.

### Decision impact
- Reduces ambiguity and encourages concrete revisit conditions.

---

## D. Action Playbooks with bounded rules (P0)

### Why it matters
- Posture without actions is a label, not a decision system.

### Product shape
- For each posture, publish compact rule blocks:
  - **Action**
  - **Threshold**
  - **Reversal condition**
- Example: "Pause net headcount growth if runway < 18 months; reverse when runway > 24 months and NRR > 110%."

### Decision impact
- Turns macro signal into operating behavior in finance, hiring, and GTM.

---

## E. Personalization layer (P1)

### Why it matters
- This is where utility jumps from "interesting" to "indispensable".

### Product shape
- Inputs: ARR, growth rate, burn multiple, runway, stage, team size.
- Output pair:
  - **Macro posture** (market-level)
  - **Your posture** (company-adjusted)
- Explain the delta in one sentence.

### Decision impact
- Users get tailored posture tension (e.g., market expansion, company defensive).

---

## F. Founder Risk Check (P1, growth lever)

### Why it matters
- Highly shareable and discussion-triggering.
- Creates a memorable artifact beyond a static dashboard.

### Product shape
- Lightweight assessment with 4–6 metrics.
- Output includes:
  - risk flags (e.g., overstaffed, undercapitalized)
  - suggested operating band
  - one immediate corrective action

### Decision impact
- Encourages immediate recalibration and social distribution.

---

## G. Shareable artifacts (P1)

### Why it matters
- Transmission is core to adoption in founder workflows.

### Product shape
- One-click export card with:
  - date
  - macro posture
  - personalized posture
  - 2 key drivers
  - 1 recommended action
- Optimize for Slack, X, and LinkedIn previews.

### Decision impact
- Increases recurring ritual use and organic growth.

---

## 2) What to REMOVE

## A. Long explanatory text on primary screens

- Move heavy explanation behind "Method" or "Why this changed" details.
- Keep homepage focused on decision delta.

## B. Dashboard sprawl

- Avoid adding many equal-weight widgets.
- If a module does not change a decision this week, demote or remove it.

## C. Generic advice copy

- Replace broad language with bounded operating instructions.
- Every recommendation should include a measurable trigger.

---

## 3) What to CHANGE

## A. Homepage IA → single weekly operating brief

Recommended order of blocks:
1. **Current posture** (headline)
2. **Confidence + trend + freshness**
3. **Primary drivers**
4. **What to do this week**
5. **What would flip the call**
6. **Historical timeline**

This creates terminal-like clarity and screenshot readiness.

---

## B. Add Startup Climate Index (P1)

### Why it matters
- A stable index gives users a shorthand for market state.

### Product shape
- Show `Score / 100`, trend arrow, and 3–4 sub-scores.
- Keep the index subordinate to the posture call (index supports, posture decides).

---

## C. Design for artifact transmission by default

- Every primary module should be copyable, citeable, and screenshot-safe.
- Add a compact citation footer with update date and data freshness status.

---

## 4) Prioritized rollout plan

## Phase 1 (2–3 weeks): credibility and decision clarity
- Historical timeline
- Confidence + trend
- Driver transparency
- Bounded playbooks on homepage

## Phase 2 (2–4 weeks): stickiness and distribution
- Personalized posture
- Founder Risk Check
- Share card export

## Phase 3 (optional): index refinement and deeper integrations
- Startup Climate Index calibration
- Additional export templates for board/leadership artifacts

---

## 5) Success metrics to track

- Weekly returning users viewing posture + actions
- Share/export rate per active user
- % sessions that open timeline or drivers
- % personalized sessions that trigger a concrete action recommendation
- 4-week retention of users who run Founder Risk Check

---

## 6) Product guardrails

To keep whether.work strong as it grows:
- Do not add new routes unless they strengthen the homepage brief.
- Do not add indicators that do not influence a decision recommendation.
- Prefer one-screen compression over explanation depth on default views.
- Ensure every recommendation has threshold and reversal logic.
